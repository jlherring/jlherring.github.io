A Little Bit About Me

Analytical and Innovative

Hello there! My name is Jenny Herring. As a southern girl who grew up making mud pies and sword fighting with sticks, the natural world is my happy place. In my work, I aim to ensure the open wildness of swaying trees and singing crickets exists for future generations, and in my home, I strive to cultivate that same love and grounding in the outdoors within my two girls.

After completing my undergraduate work at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in Anthropology and Archaeology (go Heels!), I earned a Master’s of Geospatial Information Science & Technology from North Carolina State University. Since then, I have been putting my time and enthusiasm into tackling geospatial problems like improving water quality monitoring to compiling wildfire treatment data to tracking down where my sweet daughter left her beloved stuffed octopus.

My approach to geospatial science is built on that combination of my life experience, my work experience, my family and mentors. Successful work, successful change, balances technical demands with the needs of real people – Does my team have the skills and tools we need? Do we have trust and motivation (both among ourselves and with our client)? What solution not only serves the client’s needs but is practical and realistic? I thrive on this combination of solutioning and interpersonal understanding.

I am a mother, a manager, and an overthinker, always looking for ways to improve. I look to smooth the edges with a walk outside, with reading and writing, with unplugged time among family.
