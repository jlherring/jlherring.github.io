Experience

Geospatial Solutioning and Project Management

Since entering the working world, my interests have continued to push me toward environmental-facing geospatial projects. I enjoy working with teams and initiatives that share my desire to improve how we interact with the landscape and aim to lessen the taxing human footprint. My breadth of work has introduced me to the many and varied applications of GIS, including:

  • Mapping of drinking water and wastewater resources for water quality improvement
  • Identifying upstream and downstream impacts of PFAS/PFOA contamination on water systems
  • Tracking contamination on abandoned uranium mines
  • Supporting the cleanup of hazardous household waste after natural disasters like wildfire and typhoon events
  • Improving data sharing for federally funded wildfire treatment data
I have acted in many roles - geospatial analyst, developer, database administrator, IT infrastructure support, Scrum Master, good cop, bad cop, shoulder to cry on - whichever hat was required and fit my head at the time. My experience has been mostly within the Esri ecosystem, extending to custom and open-source tools, as appropriate. While at Innovate, I helped propel the organization toward a number of Esri certifications, including the Hub Speciality, and participated in early adopter programs for products like Indoors and Tracker. I thoroughly enjoy learning new ways to build, to lead, and to leave the world a little better than I found it.

Account Request Tool

The Account Request Tool is the single most widely used ArcGIS Online and Esri Enterprise account provisioning tool at EPA. A web-based tool, it provides transparency during mass requests, easy access request tracking, and mitigates duplicative accounts.

ReSHAPE TWIG Viewer

The Treatment and Wildfire Interagency Geodatabase (TWIG) Viewer is a custom application used to compile and display information on fuel treatment projects and wildfires at the national level. The Viewer is part of the SWERI's larger initiative, the ReSHAPE Program.

TWIG In Scientific Data

The SWERI team published its first journal article about the TWIG Viewer, "A new geodatabase of fuel treatments across federal lands in the USA ," in August 2025. The article highlights the methods and logic used to compile wildfire treatment data.
